# Cardiff Live Social Needs Map — v0.2 prototype

This is a working prototype of one module of the Community Impact Accelerator, built around the Wales social mobility coalition's Phase 1 work. It exists to make a proposition pokeable in a browser rather than only on paper.

v0.2 reshapes the prototype around a needs-led entry point. You start by picking who you are (community group or employer) and what you're looking for (deprivation, organisations, or workstreams). The page nudges itself to suit. The deprivation choropleth is now switchable across the seven WIMD 2025 domains. Employers join the dataset as first-class opportunity organisations. A new Opportunity Snapshot panel summarises the labour market for a selected area. The bottom panel reframes as Emerging gaps and potential workstreams.

## What this is

A clickable demonstration of what the Live Social Needs Map (Module 1 of the Accelerator's three modules) could feel like for a coalition member, funder or employer looking at Cardiff. You can:

* See Cardiff's deprivation pattern at LSOA level, drawn from real WIMD 2025 data — and switch between overall deprivation and any of the seven contributing domains
* See community organisations and employers pinned to where they work, filterable by organisation type, geography, journey stage and service type
* Click an organisation to see what they do, who they support, the barriers they address, and where they sit in the coalition's J1–J6 framework
* Read community insight from frontline organisations, pinned to specific places
* See where the data suggests emerging gaps, and how those gaps could turn into practical workstreams
* For a selected area, see an Opportunity Snapshot covering labour-market shape, sector strengths, major employers and illustrative vacancy counts
* Imagine submitting your own insight, exporting a snapshot for a meeting, or using the picture to coordinate

The proposition this prototype is testing: that a live, shared, place-based picture — combining open data, organisational activity and lived community insight — is the right starting point for Phase 2 of the Wales work, and the right wedge for a wider place-based platform.

## What this is not

* **Not a working tool.** Nothing you do here persists. Submitting an insight closes the form; exporting a briefing produces a placeholder. This is a demonstration, not a service.
* **Not real coalition data, in places.** Where real organisational data from the Phase 1 ecosystem map has been provided, it's used. Where it hasn't, the prototype uses publicly known Cardiff organisations as placeholders. Indicators are real where StatsWales and Public Health Wales make them easy to find; flagged as illustrative otherwise. The ten v0.2 employer additions (Hodge, Principality, L&G, BBC Cymru Wales, Hugh James, Cardiff Bus, Confused.com, IQE, Dŵr Cymru, Reach) are real Cardiff businesses; their J3/J4/J5 role mappings are inferred and visibly badged "illustrative" wherever they appear. The same convention covers the three stubbed workstreams and the Opportunity Snapshot fields.
* **Not a service-area map.** The 42 pins are organisations mapped to their registered HQ. Several are Wales-wide or UK-wide bodies whose Cardiff office is one of many delivery sites. The empty space in Splott, Ely, Llanrumney and other outer wards is therefore the headquarters picture, not the activity picture — and the contrast against the dark-red WIMD polygons in those same wards is itself worth naming.
* **Not the whole platform.** The Community Impact Accelerator tech spec describes three modules: a Live Social Needs Map, a Collaboration Engine, and an Impact Ledger. This prototype is the first one only. The other two are sketches in the spec, not in this prototype.
* **Not a product roadmap.** This shows what one module could feel like. It does not commit anyone to building it, fund it, or run it.

## What it's useful for

Looking at the prototype is a faster way to react to the proposition than reading the white paper or the tech spec. Specifically, it's worth asking:

* Does a live shared picture, in this shape, feel like the right starting point for Phase 2?
* Are these the right axes — theme, population, organisation type, activity — for filtering? Or should the white paper's J1–J6 journey-stage frame lead instead?
* What's missing that would matter to a coalition member, a funder, a council commissioner?
* What would change if the public sector and corporate layers were as fully populated as the VCSE one?
* Is the community insight design treatment right, or does youth voice need its own structural treatment rather than being one of several insight types?

The questions worth raising are about the proposition. The prototype's polish, performance and visual choices are not the conversation.

## Provenance

Built from:

* The Phase 1 white paper "Accelerating Social Mobility in Wales" (April 2026)
* The Community Impact Accelerator Product & Technical Specification v0.1
* Cardiff LSOA boundaries: ONS Open Geography Portal (LSOA 2021 EW)
* Welsh Index of Multiple Deprivation 2025: StatsWales (overall rank plus all seven domain ranks)
* Place indicators: StatsWales, Public Health Wales (where available)
* Organisational data: Phase 1 ecosystem map where supplied; public sources otherwise

## What you cannot do here

* Sign in
* Save anything
* Add a real organisation
* Submit a real insight that will be reviewed
* Export a real report
* See data for places other than Cardiff

These are all in scope for a real Phase 2 build. They are deliberately not in this prototype.
